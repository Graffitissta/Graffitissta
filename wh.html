WoodHorizon

From Graduation Project to Brand Concept
My graduation project originally started under the name Drys, focusing on creating a unique e-commerce concept for handcrafted wooden creations. The initial logo depicted the natural wood grain, symbolizing the uniqueness of each piece, much like a fingerprint. This project allowed me to explore how visual identity and design can convey authenticity and craftsmanship from the very first concept.

From Project Concept to WoodHorizon
As the project evolved, it transformed into a complete website and brand, changing its name to WoodHorizon. The new logo represents an olive tree, reflecting that most creations are made from olive wood and honoring the creator’s roots in Crete—my father, who is the artisan behind these pieces. I designed the full UI/UX of the online shop using Adobe XD, focusing on simplicity, warmth, and usability, while also developing a consistent brand identity including visual guidelines and promotional materials. This project allowed me to combine my passion for design with strategic thinking, creating a digital and visual experience that truly reflects the heritage, authenticity, and craftsmanship of the brand.
